How Lean Six Sigma Transformed a Small Café’s Operations

A Brew Bliss Café case study on reducing variability, not just speed

Brew Bliss Café in Pune was built on passion and customer experience. As footfall increased, operational cracks began to appear. Customers complained about long waits during peak hours, inconsistent coffee taste, and unpredictable service times. Despite staff working hard, outcomes varied.

The core issue was not effort. It was process instability.

Using the DMAIC methodology of Lean Six Sigma, a structured improvement project was launched to address service delays without adding staff, changing the menu, or redesigning the café layout.

Define Phase: Customer feedback was captured through Voice of Customer analysis, revealing that speed and consistency were must-have expectations. A SMART goal was defined: reduce average coffee delivery time to under five minutes during peak hours without compromising quality. A SIPOC map clarified process scope and ownership.

Measure Phase: Data was collected for each order from placement to delivery. Baseline analysis showed an average service time of 4.82 minutes, but with high variability. Service times ranged from just over two minutes to more than ten. The process sigma level was only 0.12, confirming an unreliable system.

Analyze Phase: Value Stream Mapping revealed significant non-value-added time spent searching for ingredients, handling special orders, and correcting mistakes. Fishbone and Pareto analysis showed that missing cutlery, unsorted ingredients, and special order confusion caused over 75 percent of delays. Five-Why analysis traced these issues back to lack of standard routines and visual controls.

Improve Phase: A Kaizen Blitz redesigned special order handling using visual cues and standardized steps. The 5S methodology was applied to ingredient and cutlery stations, introducing labeling, visual layouts, and setup checklists. These changes simplified work instead of adding pressure.

Control Phase: Standard operating procedures, daily audits, and control charts were introduced to sustain improvements. Post-implementation data showed average delivery time reduced to 3.99 minutes, variability dropped sharply, and sigma level improved to 3.36. The process became stable and predictable.

This case demonstrates a critical lesson for small businesses: average performance hides instability, but consistency builds trust. Brew Bliss Café did not grow by working harder. It grew by designing better systems.
